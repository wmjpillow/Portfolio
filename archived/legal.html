Design Challenge -
Accelerating the Reporting Function

Due to the complicated nature of the legal industry, it is crucial that ClariLegal offers straightforward communication tools if it is to be successful in facilitating such detailed and intensive information. However, ClariLegal lacks flexible and cohesive reporting tools for both vendor and clients.

Evaluating the Current Platform

Interviews

We conducted 5 interviews of our clients and users of Clarilegal, and they provided us some very useful feedback.

Affinity Wall

We interpreted the interviews and distilled key findings after building the affinity diagram.

Heuristic Evaluation

We did heuristic evaluations for the existing platform to spot current issues.

Key Findings

  • Current discussion panel is not efficient
    Every word matters in the legal world. ClariLegal needs to create a functionality where chat transcripts between buyers and vendors can be organized and readily accessible for future reference.
  • The statement of work is not editable
    Currently, once one is made there is no ability to edit. In the legal world, this is incredibly limiting, as things are always changing. ClariLegal needs to allow and present changes to work criteria in flexible yet easily traceable manner.
  • The service comparison between vendors is not intuitive
    Law firms need ClariLegal to be their discriminating eye when it comes down to vendor offers. ClariLegal needs a tool that aids in making this an efficient decision making process by providing intuitive, easy to understand comparisons.
  • The visualization of project progress is not clear
    Time is money, money is time. Upper level management will always want to see the progress of a case, so ClariLegal needs better data visualization tools to easily track progress and hold vendors accountable.

UX Redesign

Based on the 4 key findings, we did UX Redesign for ClariLegal. We aim to create easy communication within ClariLegal and flexible reporting functionality to a third party.
